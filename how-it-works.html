How it really works

From your question to a
cited report — in one click.

Here's everything that happens behind the scenes when you hit Search — explained without the jargon.

01

You type your question,
we figure out the rest.

Just write your question in plain English — the same way you'd ask a friend. "What do we know about sleep deprivation and memory?" is a perfect query. ResearchFlow automatically breaks it into several focused angles to make sure nothing important gets missed.

No jargon needed Any topic, any field
Example question
"What are the effects of sleep deprivation on memory?"
Automatically explored as
sleep loss and short-term memory angle 1
chronic sleep deprivation cognitive effects angle 2
sleep and learning consolidation angle 3
02

We search four databases
at the same time.

Instead of searching one database and hoping for the best, ResearchFlow hits four of the largest academic databases simultaneously. That means broader coverage, fewer gaps, and results from medicine, computer science, physics, social sciences — all in one go. The whole search takes around 20 seconds.

03

We filter the noise,
keep only what matters.

Searching four databases can pull in a lot of irrelevant results. ResearchFlow automatically filters papers to keep only those that are actually relevant to your question. Duplicates are removed, irrelevant results are dropped, and what's left is ranked by how useful it's likely to be.

04

You get a real report,
not a list of links.

The final step brings everything together into a structured, readable report. It covers the key findings, notes where researchers agree or disagree, and cites every claim with a real paper. You can read it immediately, copy it, or use it as a starting point for deeper research. Every citation links back to the original source.

Common Questions

Good questions deserve good answers.

Most queries complete in 20–40 seconds. The time depends on how many sub-questions are generated and how quickly the academic APIs respond. Complex, multi-domain questions can occasionally take up to a minute.

ResearchFlow only retrieves metadata and abstracts, which are freely available through these APIs. It does not download or reproduce full-text papers from paywalled journals. The report is synthesized from publicly accessible information.

Citations are pulled directly from the academic APIs — they're not hallucinated. Each referenced paper has a real DOI or URL. That said, always verify any paper before including it in formal academic work, as database metadata can occasionally contain errors.

ResearchFlow works best for empirical, scientific, or technical questions — things with existing academic literature. "What are the effects of X on Y?" or "What does research say about Z?" are ideal. Very recent events (last few months) may have limited coverage depending on how quickly APIs index new papers.
